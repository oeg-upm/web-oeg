The Ontology Engineering Group is at the top-ten of research groups at Universidad Politécnica de Madrid (UPM). It is based on the School of Computer Science at UPM. Its research areas cover Ontological Engineering, Semantic Web and Linked Data, Semantics in Open Science, and Data-Driven Language Technologies. The OEG offers 8 positions available for the creation of the Spanish Data Space Incubator (INESData): project manager, business developer, data scientists, knowledge graph engineers and computational linguists. Deadline: 15-03-2023

BASIC INFO

PROJECT: INESData: Infraestructura para la INvestigación de ESpacios de DAtos distribuidos en UPM

ABOUT US: The Ontology Engineering Group is one of the leading research groups at Universidad Politécnica de Madrid (UPM). It is part of the larger UPM’s Research Center on Artificial Intelligence (AI.nnovation Space). We carry out fundamental and applied research in the areas of Ontological Engineering, Knowledge Graphs, Semantics in Open Science, and Data-Driven Language Technologies, in a wide range of domains. Our group is highly multicultural, formed by over 40 people from more than 10 countries worldwide, including master and PhD students, postdocs and lecturers, with backgrounds in different disciplines: computer science, maths, linguistics and social science.

NUMBER OF POSITIONS AVAILABLE: 8

CONTEXT AND MISSION: Together with the Information Processing and Telecommunications Center (IPTC) at UPM, we have recently obtained 5M€ funding from the Spanish UNICO-CLOUD programme with the mission to create one of the first Spanish incubators of data spaces. Our project, INESData - Infraestructura para la INvestigación de ESpacios de DAtos distribuidos en UPM - Infrastructure for the Research in Data Spaces at UPM, will setup the governance schemes and technical infrastructure for the deployment of data spaces in varied domain. INESData will deploy several building blocks to support data space generation (ontology maintenance, data exchange, knowledge graph maintenance, natural language processing services, linguistic resources, identity management, machine learning models, etc.). Furthermore, a Data Space Support Center will be put in place for Spanish organisations interested in setting up data sharing models in specific domains (following GAIA-X and IDSA principles). Of particular interest will be the the creation of a language data space for all languages in Spain, and at least two domain-specific data spaces in the domains of mobility and media. We expect this Data Space Support Center to incubate data spaces in additional domains in the upcoming year. To achieve our mission, we are now hiring. If you would like to involve in this high-impact initiative as well as a be part of this thriving research and engineering environment, please send your CV to ofertas@delicias.dia.fi.upm.es
