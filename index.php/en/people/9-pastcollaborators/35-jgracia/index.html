Dr. Jorge Gracia is a postdoctoral researcher at the Ontology Engineering Group (OEG) of the Artificial Intelligence Department at Universidad Politécnica de Madrid (UPM) since 2009. He obtained his degree in Physical Science and his PhD in Computer Science (2009) at University of Zaragoza.

From 1998 to 2003, he worked at companies belonging to the Accenture group and the Telecom Italia group, taking part in software engineering projects for important telecommunications and service firms (Retevisión, Auna, Danone). From 2004 to 2009, Jorge worked at the University of Zaragoza, teaching and researching as part-time lecturer and as ayudante (Assistant). During that period where he was part of the Distributed Information System group, under the supervision of Dr. Eduardo Mena.

His research belongs to the field of the Semantic Web, to which he contributed with his PhD thesis on "Integration and Disambiguation Techniques for Semantic Heterogeneity Reduction on the Web". His research experience has focused on semantic measures, ontology matching, and disambiguation techniques in the field of the Semantic Web. His current research topic is multilingualism on the Web of Data. In particular, he is exploring the migration of multilingual Language Resources (lexica, dictionaries, corpora,…) from their data silos into the linked data cloud, in order to make them interoperable and to support a future generation of (Linked Data-aware) Natural Language Processing tools. He is also interested on methods and techniques for cross-lingual linking and cross-lingual information access.

He has been research visitor at Knowledge Media Institute (Open University, United Kingdom), INRIA (France), and Università di Roma “La Sapienza”. He has worked in several European projects: Dynalearn, Monnet, and currently in LIDER, where he has the role of Quality Assurance Coordinator.

You can find out more about Jorge Gracia on his website.
